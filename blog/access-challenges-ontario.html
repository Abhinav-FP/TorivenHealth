Access to Care in Ontario: The Gap Between Knowing and Getting Help

March 2026 3 min read Launch Edition
A
Amit Bansal
Co-Founder, Toriven Health
← Back to Blog

Ontario has built meaningful infrastructure to support children and families navigating developmental and mental health challenges. The Ontario Autism Program is one example — a genuine commitment to ensuring children with autism get the support they need. We are fortunate to have it.

But having a program and being able to navigate it are two different things. The gap between knowing help exists and actually accessing it remains one of the most overlooked challenges in Ontario's health system. Waitlists stretch into years. Families are handed a diagnosis and left to piece together what comes next. Clinicians work in silos, each with their own record, their own waitlist, their own intake process.

"Access is not just a word. Early and timely support produces meaningfully better outcomes — the research is consistent on this. Every month on a waitlist is not neutral time."

I have spent two decades working across healthcare systems in Australia, the United States, and Canada — improving clinical quality, leading system integration, and watching what happens when care is coordinated versus fragmented. The differences are not subtle. Coordinated, early access changes trajectories.

Toriven Health was built on a single conviction: that no Ontario family should have to navigate a fragmented system at the moment they are most in need of clarity. We bring regulated clinicians across psychology, therapy, speech-language pathology, and occupational therapy together under one coordinated platform — virtual-first, province-wide, and built around the family, not the system.

We are not yet open. But we are close. And when we are, access will be the first thing we deliver.
