Hamilton & The Urban Corridor

Urban access — that stops
at the city limits.

Kingston has Queen’s University, KGH, and real institutional depth — but that doesn’t mean families here have had easy access to regulated private psychology and allied health services. The reality for most Kingston-area families has been a choice between a multi-year public waitlist and a long drive.

Families in rural Niagara — in Pelham, Wainfleet, Dunnville, or Haldimand County — face a very different reality. The nearest regulated private psychologist or BCBA may be 45–90 minutes away. Making multiple appointments per week for a child in therapy or ABA is simply not realistic when each visit requires a near two-hour round trip.

Virtual care reaches the whole region — Hamilton, Burlington, Niagara, and the rural pockets that fall between service areas. One intake. One clinical team. Available from your home, wherever in the region that is.

Why Virtual-First Reaches the Whole Corridor

Hamilton-quality care.
Haldimand living room.

Toriven Health is virtual-first by design — not as a compromise, but because it’s genuinely better for families who have historically faced geography as a barrier to good clinical care.

The same regulated psychologist, the same BCBA, the same SLP available to a Hamilton family — is available to a Dunnville family with the same phone and internet connection. No travel. No commute. No weekly reorganisation of the family schedule around a clinic appointment.

In-person appointments are arranged when clinically necessary. That determination is made by your clinician, on clinical grounds, before you commit to anything.

Ontario Autism Program

Understanding OAP & Private Services

A note for families with OAP funding

Ontario’s Autism Program (OAP) is one of the most important — and most misunderstood — funding pathways for families of autistic children in Eastern Ontario. With over 87,000 children on the provincial waitlist and average wait times exceeding five years, many Kingston-area families are navigating OAP funding while also accessing private regulated services.

Toriven Health is a private provider. OAP funding is administered through regional lead agencies and is not processed directly through our clinic. Families with OAP funding should contact their regional lead agency to understand how and whether funding can be directed toward private ABA and behaviour services.

We provide all clinical documentation, reports, and receipts needed to support your OAP or extended benefits funding applications. Our BCBAs are CPBAO-registered and our services meet evidence-based clinical standards.

Common Questions

Questions from Hamilton-region families

We’re in Haldimand / rural Niagara. Do we have to drive to Hamilton?

▼
No. Toriven Health is virtual-first — your sessions are conducted through secure video from your home. You don’t need to travel to Hamilton or anywhere else for most services. Certain assessment components may require in-person elements, which your clinician will confirm before you commit.

We’re already on a Hamilton clinic waitlist. Can we start private services now?

▼
Yes. Being on a public or private waitlist doesn’t prevent you from accessing care elsewhere. Many families maintain waitlist positions while beginning private regulated services. We provide full clinical documentation that can be shared with other providers.

We live in Grimsby / Beamsville / Pelham — are we within your catchment?

▼
Yes. Toriven Health serves all of Ontario virtually, and we specifically serve the Hamilton–Niagara–Haldimand corridor. Grimsby, Beamsville, Pelham, Wainfleet, Dunnville, Caledonia, Cayuga, Brantford, and all surrounding communities are within our virtual catchment.

Do you need a referral?

▼
No. You contact us directly — no GP or paediatrician referral required to begin. Some extended health benefit plans may require one for reimbursement; check your plan. We provide all receipts and documentation.

Will my extended health benefits cover services?

▼
Services are private pay and not covered by OHIP. Many extended health benefit plans cover services delivered by regulated clinicians. Coverage depends on your specific plan. Confirm with your insurer before starting. We provide itemised receipts after each session.

We have OAP funding. Can we use it here?

▼
OAP funding is administered through regional lead agencies and is not processed through Toriven Health. Contact your regional lead agency to understand how funding can be directed to private providers. We provide all documentation and receipts. Our BCBAs are CPBAO-registered.
