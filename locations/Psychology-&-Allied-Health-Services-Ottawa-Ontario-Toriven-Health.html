Ottawa & Eastern Ontario

Ontario’s capital — with the same
wait time problem as everywhere else.

Kingston has Queen’s University, KGH, and real institutional depth — but that doesn’t mean families here have had easy access to regulated private psychology and allied health services. The reality for most Kingston-area families has been a choice between a multi-year public waitlist and a long drive.

The bilingual dimension makes Ottawa’s situation uniquely complicated. Families requiring French-language services for children in French immersion or francophone schools face an even smaller pool of regulated clinicians. The Entry to School program, IEP advocacy, and early childhood assessments are all areas where French-language demand significantly exceeds supply.

Toriven Health’s virtual-first model reaches Ottawa families — and the communities surrounding Ottawa — without adding to the city’s in-person clinical burden. The same regulated psychologist, the same bilingual-capable SLP, the same BCBA: available from your home in Orleans, Kanata, Almonte, or Smiths Falls.

Bilingual Services & Ottawa Families

Psychology in the language
your family thinks in.

that most clinicians don’t fully understand: the rhythm of postings, deployments, school transitions, and the quiet weight of a child whose care gets interrupted every two to three years.

The consequences are real: assessments conducted in a second language produce less reliable results. Children assessed in English when they think primarily in French may perform differently on standardised tests, leading to under- or over-identification of learning differences. For Ottawa families, this is not a theoretical concern — it is a documented clinical risk.

Ontario Autism Program

Understanding OAP & Private Services

A note for families with OAP funding

Ontario’s Autism Program (OAP) is one of the most important — and most misunderstood — funding pathways for families of autistic children in Eastern Ontario. With over 87,000 children on the provincial waitlist and average wait times exceeding five years, many Kingston-area families are navigating OAP funding while also accessing private regulated services.

Toriven Health is a private provider. OAP funding is administered through regional lead agencies and is not processed directly through our clinic. Families with OAP funding should contact their regional lead agency to understand how and whether funding can be directed toward private ABA and behaviour services.

We provide all clinical documentation, reports, and receipts needed to support your OAP or extended benefits funding applications. Our BCBAs are CPBAO-registered and our services meet evidence-based clinical standards.

Common Questions

Questions from Ottawa families

Do you offer services in French?

▼
Yes. Toriven Health offers clinical services in both English and French. For assessments conducted in French, we ensure the clinician delivering the service has the language proficiency to provide a reliable, accurate clinical picture. Please indicate your language preference when booking your free consultation.

We’re on a public waitlist in Ottawa. Can we access private services in parallel?

▼
Yes. There is no conflict between being on a public waitlist and accessing private regulated services. Many Ottawa families maintain their position on the public waitlist while accessing private care to get support started sooner. We provide all clinical documentation that can be shared with public providers if and when a public placement becomes available.

We live in Carleton Place / Almonte / Smiths Falls. Are we within your catchment?

▼
Yes. Toriven Health serves all of Ontario virtually. Carleton Place, Almonte, Smiths Falls, Perth, Kemptville, Rockland, Arnprior, Renfrew, Pembroke, Brockville, and all surrounding communities are within our virtual catchment. No travel to Ottawa required.

Do you need a referral from a doctor or paediatrician?

▼
No. You contact us directly — no GP or paediatrician referral required. Some extended health benefit plans may require a referral for reimbursement; check your plan directly. We provide all receipts and clinical documentation to support your benefit claims.

Will my extended health benefits cover services?

▼
Services are private pay and not covered by OHIP. Many extended health benefit plans cover services delivered by regulated clinicians — coverage depends on your specific plan. Please confirm coverage directly with your insurer before starting. We provide itemised receipts after each session for reimbursement.

We have OAP funding. Can we use it with Toriven Health?

▼
OAP funding is administered through regional lead agencies and is not processed directly through Toriven Health. Families with OAP funding should contact their regional lead agency to understand how funding can be directed toward private regulated providers. We provide all documentation and receipts to support your applications. Our BCBAs are CPBAO-registered.
