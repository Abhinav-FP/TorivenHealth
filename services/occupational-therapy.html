What Is Occupational Therapy?

OT is about living fully — not just functioning.

Occupational therapy is the clinical discipline focused on helping people do the things that matter in their daily lives — what clinicians call "occupations": playing, learning, eating, dressing, sleeping, connecting with others. When any of those are hard, OT finds out why and builds a path forward.

For children, that usually means the nervous system, motor development, sensory processing, or the routines and environments that either support or undermine their ability to participate in life. For teens, it often means independence, school demands, and the executive function underpinning all of it.

OT doesn't change who your child is. It changes how much their environment and nervous system get in the way of who they already are.

🧠

Sensory Processing

How the brain receives, organizes, and responds to sensory input — touch, sound, movement, taste, sight. When this is dysregulated, daily life is exhausting.

🤲

Motor Development

Fine motor skills for hands and writing, gross motor for balance and coordination, and the body awareness that connects both.

🔄

Self-Regulation

The ability to manage arousal, emotions, and behaviour in response to what's happening — the foundation everything else is built on.

🏠

Daily Life Skills

Dressing, feeding, sleep, grooming, and the routines that either flow or fall apart — OT makes them work in your actual home.

Our Care Model
We don't run a clinic your child attends. We build the strategies into your family's real life.

Your Home Is the Clinic

Virtual OT means we see your child in the environment where the challenges actually happen — the kitchen, the bedroom, the morning routine. A sensory strategy that works at a clinic but fails at home is not a strategy. We build for your real life.

Parents Are the Intervention

Between sessions, you are the most powerful OT tool your child has. Our clinicians teach you the strategies, help you embed them into daily routines, and adjust them as your child grows. The goal is a family that doesn't need us forever.

Built for Generalization

Skills learned in sessions need to show up in real life — at school, at the dinner table, at a birthday party. Parent coaching is built into every session so that what your child learns doesn't stay in the session.

This is not drop-off therapy. You are part of every session. You are the continuity between sessions. Our OTs design the plan; your family is the environment where development happens.

At school age, sensory and motor challenges stop being easy to accommodate. Virtual OT

Why Virtual OT Actually Works

The most common hesitation families have — and why the evidence and our clinical experience point in the same direction.

The real world is where OT should happen.

The standard concern about virtual OT is understandable: "How can you do sensory work, motor activities, or hands-on therapy over a screen?" It's a fair question. And the answer changes how most people think about it.

A sensory strategy that works in a clinic but fails in your kitchen at 7am is not a strategy. A regulation routine that works in a therapy room but falls apart on the way to school has not generalized. The most powerful OT happens in the environment where the challenge actually lives — and for most families, that's home.

Virtual delivery means your OT sees your child's actual bedroom, your actual morning routine, the actual sock drawer that causes meltdowns. That context makes the intervention more relevant, not less.

For children who struggle in unfamiliar environments — which is a core feature of many sensory and regulation profiles — the clinical setting itself can be the barrier. Virtual removes it.

1

Your environment is the data

We see the actual spaces, routines, and triggers — not a clinical approximation. Every session is grounded in what's really happening at home.

2

No travel, no deregulation before the session starts

Many children with sensory or regulation challenges arrive at clinic appointments already dysregulated from the journey. Virtual eliminates that barrier entirely.

3

Parents learn in real time, in context

When a parent learns a strategy in the kitchen where they'll actually use it, carryover is dramatically better than a handout given after a clinic session.

4

Access across Ontario

Families in smaller cities, rural areas, and Northern Ontario have the same access to regulated OT as families in Toronto. Geography stops being a barrier.

Common Questions

Things Families Ask Us

Do we need a referral or a diagnosis to start OT?
No referral and no diagnosis required. Families contact us directly. If a formal assessment is needed to guide therapy, your OT will recommend one — but many families begin therapy without a formal diagnosis first.
My child has a sensory meltdown just getting to appointments. How does virtual work?
That's actually one of the strongest arguments for virtual OT. Children who dysregulate in transit, waiting rooms, or unfamiliar environments often engage far better from their own home. We design the session around where your child is regulated — which is usually where they live.
How involved do parents need to be?
Very involved — and intentionally so. You're not just observing. You're learning the strategies, practising them during the session, and applying them in daily routines between sessions. That's what makes the work generalize. Our OTs equip you to be the most effective support your child has.
Will extended health benefits cover OT?
Many extended health plans cover Occupational Therapy from registered clinicians. Coverage varies significantly by plan and employer. We provide detailed receipts formatted for insurer submission after every session. Please confirm your specific coverage with your insurer before starting.
My child needs SLP and OT. Can we do both?
Yes — and for many children, especially those with sensory, communication, and regulation needs that overlap, coordinated SLP and OT support is exactly the right model. Our team communicates across disciplines. See our Early Childhood or School-Age Program pages for what coordinated care looks like.
How many sessions will my child need?
This depends on the presenting concerns, your child's age, and how therapy progresses. Your OT gives you an honest picture at intake and reviews progress with you regularly. We don't sell packages — frequency and duration are driven by your child's clinical needs, not a predetermined program length.
