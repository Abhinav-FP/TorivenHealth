When a Focused Assessment Isn’t Enough

Is Your Situation More Complex?

If you’ve had prior assessments that haven’t given you a complete picture, need documentation for a legal or disability proceeding, or require a full cognitive profile across every domain — a Neuropsychological Battery Assessment may be what your situation requires. This is a different clinical product entirely: 40–60 hours, a 40–80 page integrated report, authored and signed by our in-house neuropsychologist.

ADHD Rarely Travels Alone

What looks like ADHD is often something more layered — anxiety that looks like avoidance, mood that looks like behaviour, effort that looks like laziness. Select your age group below.

Ages 6–10: When the Effort Is Real But the Results Don’t Follow

Young children with ADHD or attention challenges often show up as “a handful” to the outside world — but inside, they’re working incredibly hard. Here’s what families describe:

✓

Explosive reactions to transitions, homework, or small changes that seem to come out of nowhere

✓

Can focus intensely on things they love — but simply cannot start tasks they don’t

✓

Teachers raising concerns; notes home are becoming a regular occurrence

✓

Sleep is hard. Mornings are harder. The whole family day is being organized around the struggle.

✓

You’ve tried every reward chart, reminder system, and routine — and nothing holds

🧒
Ages 6–10
“The hardest part isn’t the behaviour itself. It’s not knowing whether what you’re seeing is ADHD, anxiety, development, or something else entirely — and not knowing who to ask.”
Ages 8–13: When Effort and Output Stop Matching

As academic and social demands increase, the gap between what a child is capable of and what they’re producing becomes harder to explain away. Here’s what this age group looks like:

✓

Homework that should take 20 minutes regularly takes 2 hours and ends in conflict

✓

Working harder than their classmates — but producing worse results, not better

✓

Teachers and parents are seeing different children — composed at school, falling apart at home

✓

Emotional meltdowns or shutdowns that arrive fast and are hard to recover from

✓

You’ve been told it’s “anxiety” or “ADHD” but nothing has actually changed

🎒
Ages 8–13
“This age is where masking becomes a real pattern — children holding it together at school and completely releasing at home. Understanding what’s underneath that is what changes the trajectory.”
Ages 13–17: Brilliant, Overwhelmed, and Increasingly Withdrawn

For teenagers, ADHD and mental health challenges often arrive together and are harder to separate. Common experiences at each stage

✓

Articulate and insightful in conversation — but falling behind in class

✓

Emotional storms that arrive fast, feel overwhelming, and are hard to recover from

✓

Increasingly withdrawn — activities, friendships, and interests going quiet

✓

They’ve tried therapy before — but it felt like talking without a direction

✓

They know their patterns but don’t know how to change them — and that awareness makes it worse

🎧
Ages 13–17
“For teens aged 12 and up, we offer a private feedback session — a dedicated space where your teenager hears directly from their clinician how their brain works, in language built for them.”
Adults 18+: When the Coping Strategies Stop Working

Many adults with ADHD managed for years through effort, systems, and willpower. But the strategies that worked in their twenties often stop working later. Common experiences that bring people here:

✓

You’ve always “managed” — but the coping strategies are getting harder and harder to maintain

✓

Work, relationships, or daily functioning are suffering in ways you can no longer explain away

✓

You were never assessed as a child, or a childhood diagnosis hasn’t been reviewed in years

✓

You wonder if what looks like anxiety or depression is actually something else underneath

✓

You want answers — not another strategy to layer on top of a system that’s already breaking

🧑
Adults 18+
“ADHD, anxiety, and mood challenges often overlap in ways that make each one harder to see clearly. That’s exactly why an assessment comes before any treatment plan — so the plan is actually built for you.”
Our Approach

Differential Diagnosis.
Not Just a Checklist.

Our assessments are led by Registered Psychologists (C.Psych., Ontario). We specialize in differential diagnosis — we don’t just look for ADHD. We look for what might be mimicking it, co-occurring with it, or being masked by it.

🔍

Validated Clinical Tools

We use the CPT-3, BRIEF-2, and standardized anxiety and mood measures — alongside a thorough developmental and clinical history — to build an accurate, complete picture.

🧬

Strengths-Based Profiling

We don’t just identify challenges. We document what’s working, what’s compensating, and what this person is genuinely capable of when conditions are right.

📋

School & Physician-Ready Reports

Our reports are structured for direct use by educators, pediatricians, family physicians, and workplaces. Structured in language commonly used by schools and physicians.

For Teenagers (Ages 12+)

Your Teen Gets Their Own Session

For teens aged 12 and up, we offer a private feedback session — a dedicated space where your teenager hears directly from their clinician how their brain works, in language built for them, not for a school file. Understanding yourself changes how you approach everything.

Virtual-First Advantage

We See You in Your Real Environment

Virtual delivery means we observe clients in the environment where challenges actually occur — home routines, homework time, transitions — rather than in a clinical setting where behaviour is naturally different.

Assessment methods are selected based on age, presentation, and clinical appropriateness, and administered in accordance with professional standards.

Want a clinical opinion before you decide?
Pre-Assessment Clinical Consultation

Sometimes the most useful thing before a full assessment is an honest clinical conversation. In a paid session with a registered psychologist, your clinician reviews your history and presenting concerns, explains what pathway — if any — is clinically indicated, and gives you an independent professional opinion on the right next step.

You may find that a less intensive option is right, that a phased approach makes more sense, or that a full assessment is clearly what’s needed. That clarity should come before any financial commitment — including whether a payment plan makes sense for your situation. If you do proceed with a TorivenHealth™ assessment, the consultation fee is credited in full toward your care.

What to Expect

A Clear Process, From First Call to Final Report

Every step is transparent, low-pressure, and clinically rigorous — so you always know where you are and what comes next.

1
Free 20-Min Guidance Call

A relaxed conversation. We listen to your situation and give you an honest recommendation on where to start — or let you know if something else would serve you better first. No paperwork. No commitment.

2
Clinical Intake

A deep-dive session covering history, school life, sleep, routines, and the specific challenges you’re facing. For children, parents are central. For teens and adults, the individual’s voice drives the session.

3
Assessment Battery

Validated clinical measures delivered virtually or (where clinically indicated) in person. Adapted to age and comfort level throughout. Designed to be engaging, not intimidating.

4
Feedback & Your Report

Your clinician walks you through every finding in plain language. You receive a written report built to be used directly by schools, physicians, and workplaces — with specific, actionable next steps.
